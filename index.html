At the Intersection of Healing, Learning, and Action

ECO-HOPE Initiative stands at the intersection of mental health, climate education, and creative empowerment. Through storytelling, arts, and participatory learning, it provides safe spaces where young people can express their fears, reimagine the future, and take meaningful climate action.

Its signature philosophy “Rise through climate anxiety toward sustainable, hopeful futures” continues to inspire children, educators, faith groups, and communities to rebuild their relationship with the Earth with courage and compassion.

Founder's Narrative

"Real peace is not the absence of war; it is freedom from climate anxiety."

Our founder, John Kamulegeya, believes that hope is not passive—it is action. By transforming climate anxiety into possibility, we are helping the youth move from a state of "doom" to becoming the architects of a green future.
